Reviewer 1:
1. The paper is missing works on urban micro models recently proposed on the upper mid-band like https://doi.org/10.1109/ICC52391.2025.11161884.
2. Regarding frequency selectivity, the model groups subcarriers into some contiguous tone groups and scales by \etta=\frac{N}{T} 
 under the assumption that the channel is frequency-flat at the tone-group level. It would be good to quantify the specific coherence bandwidth of the 3GPP TR 38.901 urban micro channel in this 8.2 GHz band to prove this tone group does not introduce aggregation errors. Now, if there are errors in that case, it would be good to see the loss going from flat to selective.
3. On the rank-1 model in (8), it would be good to justify the intuition behind such as spatial coupled design.
4. The user weights appearing in (12) deserve some analysis in the simulations. For instance, the impact of those weights on the mean sum-rate would be good to simulate. 


Reviewer 2:
- In the simulation setup description, the choice of the simulation parameters should be better discussed and motivated (e.g., the specific carrier frequency and bandwidth)
- Why are the users' weights in Eq. (12) chosen as uniform by default? Do the performance change for different weights?

Reviewer 3:

- What is the real novelty?

- The paper lacks suitable insights, probably due to the lack of a reference geometry.

- The coexistence between radio access at fR3 and FS over the same band is treated as a spatial multiplexing problem, but other mechanisms are possible, like using cognitive radios techniques to avoid mutual interference. I guess that FS operates on a fixed band among those allocated for FR3, thus depending on the specific cell w.r.t. FS, different bands can be used.  This would depend on overall network topology, but there is no figure illustrating that.

- Using t for subcarriers is mislead for time, in common notation.

- The proposed optimization problem shall be ran every time the UE configuration in space changes. Is it feasible in practice?


Some general comments 

- As I am sure you are aware, in currently deployed networks antenna array sizes of 64 to 128 elements are common, but 6G will likely be specified with antenna array sizes greater than 1000 elements. So it would be of interest and value to understand the performance of your approach with larger array sizes.
With regard to the number of users (10), is this the simultaneously active users or total number of registered users in a cell? When large antenna arrays are deployed, it will likely be in denser urban environments, in which case the number of registered users in a cell will likely be at least 50 or more. Again knowing the performance with a larger number of users would be of interest. Of course using larger dimensionalities may result in significantly longer simulation times, but it may be good to have as an ambition.

- As you noted the proposed algorithm is optimal under Gaussian noise assumptions, Is it expected that the deep unfolding algorithm will  also be close to optimal in the presence of fading and intercell interference?
